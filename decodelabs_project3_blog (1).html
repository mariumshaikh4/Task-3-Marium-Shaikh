Why MSME Registration Is the Baseline for Legitimate Internships

The Ministry of Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises maintains a public registry of verified organizations. When a company holds an active MSME registration, it signals financial accountability, legal compliance, and a minimum operational standard. For a B.Tech student hunting for coding internships, this single credential separates trustworthy programs from fly-by-night outfits.

What the MSME Portal Verification Actually Confirms

  • Udyam Registration Number — a unique, government-issued identifier traceable on the official portal.
  • Legal entity status — confirms the organization is registered, active, and in good standing.
  • Industry classification — verifies the company operates in the tech or services sector relevant to your internship.
  • Stipend protection — MSME-registered firms are more likely to honor stipend commitments compared to unverified startups.

The GitHub Portfolio Requirement Hiring Managers Don't Advertise

This is the insight most internship guides skip entirely. Completing an MSME registered coding internship is necessary — but it is not sufficient. The actual differentiator in 2025 hiring is the state of your GitHub repository at the time of your interview.

Hiring managers at product-focused companies have shared a consistent pattern: they evaluate three things on a GitHub profile before reading a single line of the candidate's resume.

What Hiring Managers Actually Look For in Your GitHub

  • Commit history regularity — consistent daily or weekly commits signal professional work habits, not a one-week sprint before submission.
  • README quality — a professional README with setup instructions, architecture diagrams, and a clear problem statement is treated as a writing sample.
  • Branch strategy — use of feature branches, pull requests, and merge commits demonstrates understanding of real team workflows used in backend and frontend development.
  • Issue tracking — even personal projects benefit from using GitHub Issues to log bugs and features. This signals product thinking.
  • No force-push on main — this single mistake tells a senior engineer you don't understand version control fundamentals.
